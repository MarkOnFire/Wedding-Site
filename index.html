Why the Orpheum?

We're movie theatre people. We both worked at different theatres during our teens and early twenties. We can usually be found catching up on our watch list at theatres around Madison or Chicago. The Orpheum holds a special place in our hearts: from attending Stage Door screenings and Wisconsin Film Festival movies in its heyday to comedy and music acts in its present-day resurgence, the historic space represents much of what we love about Madison past, present and future. We are excited to be part of the rebirth of the theatre and can’t wait to be a small part of its rich history.

They Met

He worked at Eastgate Cinema. She worked at Point Cinema. The fates (or perhaps upper management) intervened, and Mark came to the west side one night to babysit the staff during a company party. They had never been happier to argue over film tastes and popcorn scooping technique. It was love at first sight.

They Fell in Love

Mark and Laurie dated throughout college. They each graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison–Mark in 2010, Laurie in 2011.

Mark began a career as a writer and media producer, freelancing for the Onion, the Isthmus, a few podcasts of his own and eventually settling in as a communicator for College of Engineering at his alma mater. Laurie joined the Schools of Hope AmeriCorps program, developing the passion for educating young kids that would lead her to Chicago to become an early childhood educator. Feeling a furry hole in their hearts, the pair adopted two rescue Boston terriers: Mac the speed demon made them a trio in August 2011, and Bacon the flatulent cuddled his way into their hearts in March 2014.

They Went For It

After six years of being together, weathering career changes, a move to Chicago in 2013 and that time that Bacon wouldn’t stop pooping in the house, Mark and Laurie knew they could do anything if they did it together.

Mark dropped to his knee outside the Landmark Century Centre Cinema on January 31, 2014. There was no question in Laurie's mind.
