Mark and Laurie: The Wedding

Coming soon to the Orpheum Theatre

August 29, 2015

Hi. We're Mark and Laurie.
We're getting married on
August 29th, 2015
at the Orpheum Theatre.

What wedding?

Pretty crazy, right? We’ve been dating for almost eight years and we decided it might just make sense to get married.

When does it start?

The ceremony kicks off at 4:30 PM, followed by cocktails from 5:30 to 7:00 PM. Dinner starts at 7:00 PM. Dancing starts at 8:00 PM. Party ends when they kick us out.

Parking?

We’re recommending you park in the State Street Capitol Garage (214 N. Carroll St). You’ll pay a flat rate of $5 for overnight parking there. Parking for overnight Concourse guests is $10 per night.

Uh, beds?

We’ve booked a block of rooms for wedding guests at the Madison Concourse Hotel and Governor's Club. Call 800-356-8293 and mention “Gebhard/Riechers Wedding Rooms” by 7/31 to receive special rate of $164 per night.

What if I get there early?

The Orpheum is near all of State Street's dining and shopping destinations, the Farmers' Market, and the University of Wisconsin-Madison campus.

You can also check out Tone for exciting ideas on how to spend your day.

Why the Orpheum?

We are movie theatre people. We both worked at different theatres during our teens and early twenties. We can usually be found catching up on our watch list at theatres around Madison or Chicago.The Orpheum holds a special place in our hearts: from attending Stage Door screenings and Wisconsin Film Festival movies in its heyday to comedy and music acts in its present-day resurgence, the historic space represents much of what we love about Madison past, present and future. We are excited to be part of the rebirth of the theatre and can’t wait to be a small part of its rich history.

Sound fun? RSVP to attend!

Synopsis

They Met

He worked at Eastgate Cinema. She worked at Point Cinema. The fates (or perhaps upper management) intervened, and Mark came to the west side one night to babysit the staff during a company party. They had never been happier to argue over film tastes and popcorn scooping technique. It was love at first sight.

They Fell in love

Mark and Laurie dated throughout college. They each graduated from the University of Wisconsin-Madison–Mark in 2010, Laurie in 2011.

Mark began a career as a writer and media producer, freelancing for the Onion, the Isthmus, a few podcasts of his own and eventually settling in as a communicator for College of Engineering at his alma mater. Laurie joined the Schools of Hope AmeriCorps program, developing the passion for educating young kids that would lead her to Chicago to become an early childhood educator. Feeling a furry hole in their hearts, the pair adopted two rescue Boston terriers: Mac the speed demon made them a trio in August 2011, and Bacon the flatulent cuddled his way into their hearts in March 2014.

The Went For It

After six years of being together, weathering career changes, a move to Chicago in 2013 and that time that Bacon wouldn’t stop pooping in the house, Mark and Laurie knew they could do anything if they did it together.

Mark dropped to his knee outside the Landmark Century Centre Cinema on January 31, 2014. There was no question in Laurie's mind.

Cast

Mark Riechers

Mark, a soon to be 29-year-old writer and media guy, likes to play video games and drink dark beers. October is his favorite month because it’s a convenient excuse to watch 31 horror movies in a row. He loves to listen to indie bands, but will not apologize for his karaoke picks: “Out of Africa” by Toto, “Stronger” by Kelly Clarkson and “Shake it Off” by Taylor Swift. Laurie Gebhard

Laurie, a 27-year-old preschool teacher, likes to binge watch shows on Netflix. She enjoys reading the occasional book when she can squeeze it into her TV schedule. Passionate about education and social justice, she can be heard ranting about politics and inequalities at all hours of the day and night when she’s not sipping on her bourbon Old Fashioned or eating her weight in dark chocolate.
